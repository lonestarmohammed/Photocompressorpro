How to Reduce Photo Size for Email (Under 1MB/2MB)

Updated: October 18, 2025 • 4–6 min read

Many email services limit attachments to a few MB and compress them anyway. The easiest fix: pre-compress your photos so they’re under 1MB or 2MB before sending—without making them blurry.

Quick method (works on phone or laptop)

  1. Open the Photo Compressor Pro homepage.
  2. Click Choose Images and pick your photos.
  3. Set Quality to 70–80% for most photos.
  4. Download and check size (target: <1MB or <2MB each).
Tip: If your photo is still large (e.g., 4000px wide), resize first using your phone’s editor, then compress.

Recommended settings

  • JPG format: Best for photos going by email.
  • Quality 75%: Great balance of size & clarity.
  • Dimensions: 1920px longest side is usually enough for viewing.

Send multiple photos in one file

Instead of attaching 10 images, send one PDF:

  1. Open Image → PDF.
  2. Select images in the order you want.
  3. Use A4 and Contain fit, then Create PDF.

Keep images clean & professional

Distracting backgrounds increase file size and reduce clarity. Use our Background Remover to erase solid backdrops and export as transparent PNG where needed.
