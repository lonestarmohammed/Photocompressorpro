Understanding Tolerance: The Key to Perfect Background Removal

Tolerance is the most important setting for getting clean results. Here's how it works:

  • Low Tolerance (10-30): Removes only colors very similar to your clicked spot. Use this when you want precise control and the background is uniform.
  • Medium Tolerance (30-60): Removes a wider range of similar colors. Ideal for backgrounds with slight variations or shadows.
  • High Tolerance (60-80): Removes most similar colors in the area. Use for complex backgrounds or when you need to remove multiple shades at once.
🔄 Best Practice: Always start with a low tolerance value and click the main background area. If some background remains, gradually increase tolerance rather than starting with a high value that might remove parts of your subject.

Professional Tips for Flawless Results

  • Use high-contrast photos: Images with clear separation between subject and background work best
  • Click multiple areas: For uneven backgrounds, click different spots to sample various colors
  • Utilize Auto-pick Corner: This feature automatically samples all four corners for complex backgrounds
  • Optimize image size first: Large images process slower - compress first for better performance
  • Check edges carefully: Zoom in to ensure clean edges around hair, fur, or detailed objects
