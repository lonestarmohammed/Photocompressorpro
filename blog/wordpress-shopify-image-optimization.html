WordPress & Shopify Image Optimization: Sizes, Formats, Compression

Slow product pages kill conversions. Here’s a practical, copy-paste guide to get blazing images on WordPress and Shopify—without losing quality.

Exact target sizes

  • Hero banners: 1600–1920px width
  • Product gallery: 1200–1500px longest side
  • Thumbnails: 300–600px width
  • Blog images: 1200px width

Best formats

  • Photos: JPG (or WebP)
  • Logos/icons with transparency: PNG or WebP
  • Screenshots/UI: WebP (smaller) or PNG for crispness

Compression that keeps quality

  • JPG: quality 75–85%
  • PNG: keep transparency; consider converting to WebP
  • WebP: visually lossless at 70–80% in most cases
Pro tip: Pre-compress before upload. Use our Image Compressor to shrink files in your browser—no uploads or watermarks.

WordPress quick wins

  • Set theme image sizes to the targets above.
  • Enable lazy-loading and responsive image srcset.
  • Replace oversized hero images and re-compress media library culprits.

Shopify quick wins

  • Upload WebP or high-quality JPGs at correct dimensions.
  • Check your theme: lazy-load & responsive images should be on.
  • Audit collection pages with many thumbnails—compress in bulk first.
